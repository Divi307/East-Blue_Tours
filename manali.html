Manali

Escape to the Himalayas: Manali Travel Package

Nestled in the breathtaking landscapes of the Himalayas, Manali is a paradise for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and peace seekers. From snow-capped mountains to lush valleys, serene rivers to thrilling activities, Manali offers an unforgettable experience for every traveler.


Package Highlights

Duration: 4 Days / 3 Nights
Best Time to Visit: March to June (for pleasant weather) and December to February (for snowfall).
Ideal For: Families, Couples, Adventure Seekers, Honeymooners


Itinerary Overview


Day 1: Arrival & Local Sightseeing

Welcome to Manali: Arrive at Bhuntar Airport or Manali Bus Stand.Transfer to your hotel.
Hadimba Temple: Visit the ancient wooden temple dedicated to Goddess Hadimba, surrounded by cedar forests.
Vashisht Hot Springs: Relax in the natural hot water springs and explore the nearby Vashisht Temple.
Evening: Stroll along the Mall Road for shopping and local cuisine.
Overnight Stay: 3-star or boutique hotel in Manali.


Day 2: Solang Valley & Adventure Activities

Solang Valley: Enjoy the stunning views of the valley and indulge in adventure activities like paragliding, zorbing, and skiing (seasonal).
Rahala Falls: Stop by this beautiful waterfall en route to Solang Valley.
Evening: Return to the hotel and relax.
Overnight Stay: 3-star or boutique hotel in Manali.


Day 3: Rohtang Pass & Sissu

Rohtang Pass: Explore the majestic high-altitude pass (subject to weather conditions and permits).
Enjoy snow activities like sledging and snowboarding.
Sissu Village: Visit this picturesque village on the way back to Manali.
Evening: Enjoy a bonfire and local Himachali dinner at the hotel.
Overnight Stay: 3-star or boutique hotel in Manali.


Day 4: Departure

Morning: Visit the Tibetan Monastery and explore the local market for handicrafts and woolens.
Departure: Transfer to Bhuntar Airport or Manali Bus Stand for your onward journey.


Inclusions:

Accommodation in a 3-star or boutique hotel.
Daily breakfast and one traditional Himachali dinner.
Private AC vehicle for sightseeing and transfers.
Guided tours with a local expert.


Exclusions:

Airfare/Train tickets.
Personal expenses and tips.
Adventure activity charges (paragliding, skiing, etc.).
Rohtang Pass permit fees (if applicable).
